Sustainability Urban Planning

Urban Heat Island Mitigation Strategies

groups
Participants

Dr. Sarah Jenkins (PI), Mark Owens (Postdoc), Elena Rostova (PhD Candidate)

Description

This longitudinal study investigates the efficacy of integrated green infrastructure in high-density urban corridors. By deploying a network of micro-climate sensors across three metropolitan areas, we are developing predictive models to assess cooling potential.

flag Objectives

  • Quantify surface temperature variance
  • Model airflow impedance of varying vegetation
  • Develop actionable zoning guidelines

analytics Results

Initial data indicates a 2.4°C average reduction in ambient temperature within 50 meters of high-density green roofing installations during peak summer months.

Water Management

Predictive Modeling of Aquifer Depletion Rates in Arid Regions

Authors: M. Chen, L. Thompson

Resumen

Utilizing machine learning algorithms on decades of hydrological data, this paper predicts accelerated aquifer depletion in specific arid zones. The model accounts for varying climate change scenarios and suggests immediate policy interventions for agricultural water usage.

Chen, M., & Thompson, L. (2023). Hydrological Resource Review, 15(2), 45-60.

Materials ScienceCircular Economy

Life-Cycle Analysis of Bio-Based Polymers in Industrial Applications

Authors: Dr. R. Gomez, H. Kim

Resumen

A comprehensive evaluation of the environmental impact of newly developed bio-polymers compared to traditional petrochemical plastics across their entire life cycle. Results indicate a 40% reduction in carbon footprint during production, though end-of-life composting infrastructure remains a challenge.

Gomez, R., & Kim, H. (2023). Sustainable Materials Today, 8(4), 210-225.
